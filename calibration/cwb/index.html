    Custom White Balance¶

    Purpose¶

    After hot mirror removal, the sensor receives red light approximately 100× more abundantly than NIR radiation. Without correction, the red channel saturates completely and the NIR channels are underexposed. Custom white balance (CWB) equalizes the three channels before each shooting session by using a fully overexposed sky reference frame.

    CWB also prevents vignetting — the differential brightness between the centre and periphery of the image that would otherwise introduce spatial variation into the measurements.

    Procedure¶

    Perform this at the start of every shooting session and repeat whenever lighting conditions change significantly (e.g. moving between open sky and under tree canopy, or when cloud cover changes).

    1. Mount the Hoya A25 filter on the lens
    2. Point the camera directly at open sky — avoid pointing at the sun
    3. Set aperture to maximum (lowest f-number available, f/4 on most lenses)
    4. Set shutter speed to approximately 30 seconds
    5. Take the photograph — all three channels should be fully saturated (pure white)
    6. Set this frame as the custom white balance reference in the camera menu

    Histogram Checking¶

    After each calibration photograph, verify exposure using the histogram:

    • Correctly exposed: histogram peaks in the centre with no clipping at either end
    • Underexposed: histogram pushed to the left — increase exposure or open aperture
    • Overexposed: histogram pushed hard against the right — reduce exposure or close aperture

    Reject and reshoot any photograph showing significant under or overexposure. Do not attempt to correct exposure in post-processing — the RAW conversion step must preserve the original linear sensor data.

    Warning

    Having removed the hot mirror, the risk of overexposure is substantially higher than with a standard camera. Always check the histogram after every shot, particularly when shooting in bright midday conditions.
