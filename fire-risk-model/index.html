    Fire Risk Model¶

    Airborne fire risk detection from multispectral imagery using a two-stage deep learning pipeline.

    Overview¶

    The system processes Red / NIR / RedEdge imagery acquired from a UAV or aircraft and produces a pixel-wise fire risk probability map. The pipeline is split into two sequential stages so that the ecology characterisation can be validated independently against SCANFI v2 ground truth before the fire risk model is trained on top of it.

    Raw bands (Red / NIR / RedEdge)
        ↓ IndicesLayer
  NDVI · NDRE · RRI2 · CIre · NIR_texture
        ↓ Stage 1 — EcologyModel
  landcover (20) · TSD (1) · crown_close (1) · species (5) · fuel_density (1)
        ↓ concat with spectral indices
        ↓ Stage 2 — AugmentedFireRiskModel
  fire risk probability map  [0, 1]

    Key features¶

    Feature Detail
    Sensor Modified Sony A7S II — R = Red 665 nm, G = RedEdge 705 nm, B = NIR 842 nm
    Spectral indices NDVI, NDRE, RRI2, CIre, NIR_texture (differentiable, computed in-network)
    Stage 1 outputs Landcover class · Time-since-disturbance · Crown closure · Species composition · Fuel density
    Stage 1 ground truth SCANFI v2 (Natural Resources Canada, 30 m, 9 time steps 1985–2025)
    Stage 2 output Per-pixel fire risk in [0, 1] with Low / Medium / High thresholds
    Uncertainty MC-Dropout over 20 forward passes → per-pixel mean + std embeddings
    Deployment target NVIDIA Jetson Orin (self-contained deploy/infer.py)

    Spectral indices¶

    Five indices are computed on-the-fly by IndicesLayer — a differentiable PyTorch layer that runs inside both models:

    Index Formula Range Fire relevance
    NDVI (NIR − Red) / (NIR + Red) [−1, 1] Low = stressed / burned vegetation
    NDRE (NIR − RedEdge) / (NIR + RedEdge) [−1, 1] Sensitive to early chlorophyll stress
    RRI2 Red / RedEdge² [0, 1] Low = healthy canopy, high = bare/burned
    CIre NIR / (RedEdge + ε) − 1, normalised [0, 1] Dense chlorophyll; less saturation than NDVI at high LAI
    NIR_texture Local std of NIR (15-px window) [0, 1] Rough = conifer; smooth = broadleaf

│   │   ├── prithvi.py          # Prithvi-100M foundation model adapter
│   │   └── satmae.py           # SatMAE ViT-L foundation model adapter
│   ├── training/
│   │   ├── trainer.py          # Training loop + checkpointing
│   │   ├── losses.py           # Fire risk losses (OHEM + FocalDice)
