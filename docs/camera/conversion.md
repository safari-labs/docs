# Camera Conversion

## Overview

The Sony A7S II contains an internal hot mirror filter (also called an IR-cut filter) that blocks near-infrared radiation to produce images similar to human vision. For full-spectrum photography, this filter must be removed and replaced with a piece of optically clear glass of the same dimensions.

Once removed, the sensor becomes sensitive to UV, visible, and NIR wavelengths simultaneously. The Hoya A25 red filter is then used externally on the lens to block UV, blue, and green wavelengths, leaving only red and NIR radiation to reach the sensor.

## Why Professional Conversion is Required

Unlike the Nikon D50 used in Patón (2020), the hot mirror in the Sony A7S II is integrated into the sensor stack rather than sitting as a discrete removable piece. DIY removal risks permanent sensor damage. Professional conversion services have the tools and experience to perform this safely and recalibrate back-focus after the conversion.

Request a **full-spectrum conversion** specifically — not an IR-only conversion, which permanently limits the camera to infrared wavelengths only. Full-spectrum preserves the ability to use external filters to select different wavelength ranges.

## What to Expect After Conversion

- The camera will no longer produce accurate colours without an external filter
- The metering system will be unreliable — manual exposure control is required for all shooting
- Back-focus will be recalibrated by the conversion service so lenses focus correctly
- NIR light focuses at a slightly different point than visible light — some chromatic aberration is normal without filtration
